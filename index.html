Critical GIS / Public Research Map

MMIP in Washington

A prototype GIS for Western Washington that layers MMIP cases with tribal, county, and historical land-act geographies to make jurisdictional fragmentation visible.

Project Focus

This map is not a conventional crime dashboard. It uses layered GIS evidence to show how MMIP cases sit inside overlapping tribal, county, and state jurisdictions shaped by earlier land dispossession and changing territorial control.

Native Land is displayed as a contextual narrative layer. It is not equivalent to a formal legal jurisdiction boundary.

Why GIS Matters

One of the most important mitigation tools in the MMIP crisis is the ability to visualize where legal responsibility, community presence, and historical land change do not line up cleanly.

Comparing case locations with tribal lands, county lines, and land acts helps researchers explain why records can disappear into gaps between agencies rather than a single accountable system.

Data Notes

The bundled data is sample data for prototyping only. Case points are generalized and should not be interpreted as precise incident locations.

Historical land-act areas are interpretive research layers for storytelling and comparison. They are not parcel-accurate legal boundaries.

Replace the sample files in the data/ folder with research-validated GeoJSON before public release.
